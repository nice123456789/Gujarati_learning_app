Gujarati Alphabet — Complete Guide
Section 01
Vowels — સ્વર (Svar)
Gujarati has 13 vowels. They can stand alone or attach to consonants as vowel signs (matras).
Tip: Gujarati does not have the horizontal line (शिरोरेखा) that Hindi/Devanagari has. The letters are rounder and slightly simpler. Click any letter to learn more.
Section 02
Consonants — વ્યંજન (Vyanjan)
34 consonants, organised in groups by where in the mouth they are produced — just like Sanskrit.
Tip: Gujarati consonants are grouped by articulation point — throat (કઠ), palate (તાળવું), teeth (દાંત) etc. Learning them in order makes pronunciation natural.
Section 03
Vowel Signs — માત્રા (Maatra)
When vowels attach to consonants, they become "matras" — small signs written around the consonant letter.
Example: ક (ka) + િ (i matra) = કિ (ki). The vowel sign wraps around the consonant.
Section 04
Numbers — અંક (Ank)
Gujarati has its own numeral system, though Arabic numerals (0–9) are also widely used today.
Section 05
Daily Conversations — વાર્તાલાપ (Vaartalaap)
Real phrases used every day in Vadodara and across Gujarat. Click any phrase to see notes and usage tips.
